Inspired by Team 987's 2024 turret, the belt-driven design enables shooting from any field position without repositioning the robot. Spacing can be adjusted by varying belt length, allowing flexible packaging.

No dedicated gearbox in the drive path — belts drive directly to the pulley, eliminating backlash and improving positional accuracy. A WCP Greyt Turret-inspired stacked bearing arrangement fully constrains and retains the turret across all axes.

Wire loom floats from hopper support bars to provide power across all 360° of movement.

Turret Iterations
V1 — Gear-Driven (Team 1717 Inspired)
Inspired by Team 1717's 2012 robot. Large-diameter gears were more manufacturable than equivalent pulleys given our tooling. Ruled out due to packaging constraints and insufficient gear reduction in the turret drive, impacting integration and controllability.
V2 — Belt-Driven (Team 987 Inspired)
Belt spacing adjustable to fit packaging requirements. WCP Greyt Turret-inspired stacked bearing arrangement provides stability and smooth rotation. Direct belt drive eliminates gearbox backlash for more consistent and responsive turret control. Final configuration throughout the season.

The shooter uses a fixed-angle hood at 25° — calculated to be optimal for shots from any distance except immediately adjacent to the hub. This eliminates the need for a variable hood mechanism, reducing complexity.

Steel flywheel gears maintain a high Moment of Inertia (8.28) to resist speed changes as each Fuel piece is fed, ensuring consistent exit velocity shot to shot.

TPU wedges inside the shooter assist in centering each piece of Fuel before it contacts the flywheel, improving shot consistency.

Combined System — Shoot From Anywhere

The turret and shooter work as a unified system allowing the robot to score while moving across the field without reorienting. Inspiration drawn heavily from Team 4481 (Rembrandts) 2026 robot Era, whose integration of indexing and turreted scoring informed many key design decisions.

1
Full 360° Coverage
Turret rotates continuously — robot never needs to face the hub to score. Driver can maintain optimal field positioning regardless of shot direction.
2
Minimal Backlash
No dedicated gearbox in the turret drive path — direct belt actuation eliminates backlash and improves positional precision for accurate shot angle control.
3
Consistent Exit Velocity
High-MOI steel flywheel gears resist speed drop-off as each ball feeds. TPU wedges center each piece for repeatable contact geometry and predictable shot.
