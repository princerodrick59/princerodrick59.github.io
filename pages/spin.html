Design Rationale

The spindexer agitates and serializes fuel into the indexer. The modular central hub was designed for rapid prototyping — minimal disassembly required between full spinner swaps, enabling 14 total iterations.

Final design uses a ¼" polycarbonate spinning plate with cat tongue grip tape on outer edges (inspired by Team 190), plus a central cone with grip tape (inspired by Team 9483) to improve Fuel movement and eliminate dead zones near the hopper center.

Spinner Iterations — Highlights
V1 — Brush Only (Inspired by 4481)
Compliant brushes performed well at low Fuel quantities with high BPS throughput. At higher storage capacities the brushes couldn't manage the load, resulting in inconsistent feeding. Not viable for target performance.
V4 — Brush + Bent Poly + Witch Hat Agitator
Hybrid system combined rigid and compliant elements. Added a 3D-printed conical "witch hat" with auger-like geometry to distribute Fuel radially and prevent clustering near center.
V9 — Rigid Plates (Week 0)
Inspired by Team 1986's 2017 Spindexer. Highly rigid 1/8" + 1/4" polycarbonate with internal plates. Consistent and durable but limited throughput — Fuel required extra time to index, resulting in lower BPS.
V13 — ¼" Spinning Plate + Cat Tongue (Post Week 0)
Inspired by Team 190's simple spinning disc design. Retained existing drive components, redesigned spinner to a polycarbonate disc with cat tongue grip tape on outer edges. More consistent indexing and improved throughput.
V14 — 9483 Style (Central Cone)
Following Week 1, Team 9483's central cone addition stood out. Added a cone with cat tongue grip tape to regulate Fuel movement, reduce dead zones, and increase control at the center of the hopper.
Tub Iterations
V1 — Welded Aluminum
Functionally effective but achieving precise, clean geometry with aluminum and available welding/laser cutting was more challenging than desired. Moved to a better material.
V2 — Welded Stainless Steel
Nearly identical geometry to V1. Steel improved manufacturability and produced cleaner fabrication. Aggressive weight reduction features incorporated to offset the higher density.
V3 — 1/32" Polycarbonate (Final)
~5 lb lighter than the steel version. Fully formed using bends with tab-and-rivet assembly. Lightweight, manufacturable, and sufficiently rigid — leverages the same bent-polycarbonate success seen across the season.
