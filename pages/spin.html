Design Rationale

The spindexer agitates and serializes fuel into the indexer. One feature incorporated from the beginning to enable rapid prototyping was an extremely modular central hub — requiring minimal disassembly to try a completely new spinner design. This is why we were able to complete as many iterations as we did.

The final design uses a ¼" polycarbonate spinning plate with cat tongue grip tape on the outer edges (inspired by Team 190), plus a central cone with grip tape (inspired by Team 9483) to improve Fuel movement and eliminate dead zones near the hopper center.

Spinner Iterations — Highlights
V1 — Brush Only (Inspired by 4481)
While following Team 4481's Open Alliance thread, we were inspired to explore a compliant brush-based solution for Fuel management. This concept leveraged flexible brushes to agitate and guide game pieces, aiming to improve flow consistency while reducing jamming.

In testing, the system performed very well at low Fuel quantities, achieving high balls-per-second (BPS) throughput due to its compliant nature. However, at the larger storage capacities required for our design, the brushes were unable to effectively manage the increased load, leading to inconsistent feeding. As a result, this approach was not viable for our target performance and was set aside in favor of more scalable solutions.
V4 — Brush + ⅛" Bent Poly + Witch Hat Agitator
In this iteration, we developed a hybrid Spindexer that combined both rigid and compliant elements to leverage the advantages of each approach. The system integrated brushes with a ⅛" bent polycarbonate structure, aiming to balance controlled geometry with compliance for improved Fuel handling and reduced jamming.

Additionally, we introduced a small 3D-printed conical feature — referred to as the "witch hat" — designed to actively distribute Fuel outward. This component incorporated an auger-like geometry, helping to move game pieces radially and prevent clustering near the center of the hopper.
V9 — Rigid ⅛" Poly + ¼" Poly + Internal Plates (Week 0)
This iteration advanced to Week Zero and was heavily inspired by Team 1986's 2017 Spindexer, emphasizing a highly rigid architecture to achieve consistent and controlled Fuel movement. The design utilized a combination of ⅛" and ¼" polycarbonate, along with internal plates, to create a durable and structurally robust system.

While the Spindexer performed reliably, its rigid nature limited overall throughput. Fuel required additional time to settle into indexed positions, resulting in lower balls-per-second (BPS) than desired. To maximize durability and stiffness, all polycarbonate components were bent to form structural features, reinforcing the system while maintaining a relatively lightweight profile.
V13 — ¼" Spinning Plate + Cat Tongue (Post Week 0)
After observing multiple Week 0 competitions, we identified Team 190's approach as a highly effective yet simple solution for indexing game pieces — a spinning polycarbonate disc located at the base of the robot. This design demonstrated strong performance with minimal complexity, aligning closely with our design philosophy.

We adapted this concept by retaining our existing spindexer drive components while implementing a redesigned polycarbonate spinner. To further enhance performance, we applied Cat Tongue grip tape along the outer edges of the disc, increasing friction and improving the transfer of motion to the Fuel. This resulted in more consistent indexing and improved overall throughput.
V14 — Central Cone (Inspired by 9483)
Following Week 1 events, one of the most standout performances came from Team 9483, who demonstrated a highly effective spindexer-based system. Their design closely resembled our architecture, with one key addition — a centrally located cone with grip tape applied to its outer surface to improve game piece control.

For our final iteration, we incorporated a similar central cone into our Spindexer to better regulate Fuel movement. This feature improved the transfer of motion from the rotating system to the game pieces, reducing dead zones and increasing overall control, resulting in more consistent and efficient indexing performance.
Tub Iterations

We developed three primary iterations of our spindexer tub, each driven by manufacturing constraints, weight considerations, and structural performance.

V1 — 20 Gauge Welded Aluminum
Our initial version was constructed from welded aluminum. While functionally effective, achieving precise, clean geometry with aluminum — given our available welding and laser cutting capabilities — was more challenging than desired, leading us to explore alternative materials.
V2 — 20 Gauge Welded Stainless Steel
The second iteration transitioned to stainless steel while maintaining nearly identical geometry. Steel allowed for improved manufacturability and cleaner fabrication; however, due to its higher density, we incorporated aggressive weight reduction features to mitigate the mass increase.
V3 — 1/32" Polycarbonate (Final)
Our final iteration utilized 1/32" polycarbonate to significantly reduce weight — achieving approximately a 5-pound reduction compared to the steel version. Leveraging our success with bent polycarbonate throughout the season, this design was fully formed using bends and assembled with tabs and rivets, resulting in a lightweight, manufacturable, and sufficiently rigid structure.
