Design Rationale

The elevator-style rigging uses constant tension springs for passive extension and Dyneema rope for retraction — simplifying the rigging and removing the need for a double-acting deployment mechanism. Inspired by Team 2231's 2024 approach.

The REV Robotics brake stage on the 45:1 planetary guarantees the climber holds position in the air without active motor power — critical for maintaining a reliable L1 at the end of the match.

Hook geometry draws from Team 1690's 2026 auto-centering design, enabling faster alignment and more consistent engagement with the rung. The 3D-printed hook is sandwiched by ⅛" aluminum sheet to reduce weight while maintaining structural integrity.

Iteration History
V1 — Pneumatic Climb (Team 2056 Inspired)
"Zero-second climb" concept using single-acting cylinders with a double-acting solenoid. Generated sufficient lift force, but the center of gravity was misaligned — robot could not fully clear the ground. Pneumatics were later identified as adding significant weight and complexity and were removed entirely from the robot, requiring a full redesign.
V2 — Elevator-Style Rigging (Final)
Constant force springs extend the mechanism; Dyneema rope and an AndyMark winch control retraction. Simpler rigging with fewer failure modes. REV Robotics brake stage ensures the robot stays suspended without motor power. 1690-inspired auto-centering hook reduces driver workload during endgame. Final configuration throughout the season.
