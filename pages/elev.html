The slap-down intake acquires fuel from the ground and fills the hopper. Foam-wrapped rollers provide compliance for consistent "touch it, own it" acquisition. The bent polycarbonate bull bar stiffens the front and transfers impacts into the structure rather than the roller.

A jackshaft ensures both intake arms move in perfect sync. The dead axle pivot removes all torsion loading from the pivot shafts and the dead axle rollers remove all shafts through the roller body, reducing both weight and failure modes.

Intake Iterations
V1 — Polybelt PVC Roller
Initial design used a PVC roller coated with polybelt material. Did not meet "touch it, own it" standards — acquisition was inconsistent. Polybelt was also an unfamiliar material, introducing uncertainty in tuning and durability.
V2 — Foam Rollers (Week 0)
Inspired by Team 254's 2025 intake. Replaced PVC with polycarbonate roller and wrapped in refrigeration-line insulation foam for compliance. Skateboard tape on the ramp increased friction. Bent polycarbonate bull bar added for front stiffness. ~8.5 lbs, passive extension.
V3 — Final (Pivot + Jackshaft)
Retained V2 geometry and added a pivoting element to agitate Fuel and ensure consistent flow out of the hopper. Jackshaft added for synchronized motion between both arms.

The spindexer agitates and serializes fuel into the indexer. The modular central hub enabled rapid prototyping — minimal disassembly required between full spinner swaps, enabling 14 total iterations.

Final design uses a ¼" polycarbonate spinning plate with cat tongue grip tape on outer edges (inspired by Team 190), plus a central cone with grip tape (inspired by Team 9483) to improve Fuel movement and eliminate dead zones.

Spindexer Iterations — Highlights
V1 — Brush Only (Inspired by 4481)
Compliant brushes performed well at low Fuel quantities with high BPS throughput. At higher storage capacities the brushes couldn't manage the load, resulting in inconsistent feeding. Not viable for target performance.
V4 — Brush + Bent Poly + Witch Hat Agitator
Hybrid system combined rigid and compliant elements. Added a 3D-printed conical "witch hat" with auger-like geometry to distribute Fuel radially and prevent clustering near center.
V9 — Rigid Plates (Week 0)
Inspired by Team 1986's 2017 Spindexer. Highly rigid 1/8" + 1/4" polycarbonate with internal plates. Consistent and durable but limited throughput — Fuel required extra time to index, resulting in lower BPS.
V13 — ¼" Spinning Plate + Cat Tongue (Post Week 0)
Inspired by Team 190's simple spinning disc design. Retained existing drive components, redesigned spinner to a polycarbonate disc with cat tongue grip tape on outer edges. More consistent indexing and improved throughput.
V14 — 9483 Style (Central Cone)
Following Week 1, Team 9483's central cone addition stood out. Added a similar cone with cat tongue grip tape to regulate Fuel movement, reduce dead zones, and increase control at the center of the hopper.
Spindexer Tub Iterations
V1 — Welded Aluminum
Functionally effective but achieving precise, clean geometry with aluminum and available welding/laser cutting was challenging. Moved to a better material.
V2 — Welded Stainless Steel
Nearly identical geometry to V1. Steel improved manufacturability and produced cleaner fabrication. Aggressive weight reduction features incorporated to offset higher density.
V3 — 1/32" Polycarbonate (Final)
~5 lb lighter than the steel version. Fully formed using bends with tab-and-rivet assembly. Lightweight, manufacturable, and sufficiently rigid — leverages the same bent-polycarbonate success seen across the season.

1792 TubeRoller System

A custom standardized dead-axle roller system used on every roller of the robot. Goal: a 3D-printable, competition-durable roller compatible with PVC or polycarbonate tubes, fully parametric for any robot.

How It Works

Structural Bolt (Not a Shaft)
A ¼-20 bolt acts as the structural member instead of a traditional dead axle shaft — significantly reducing weight and eliminating shaft bending risk under load.
Full-Length Internal Spacer
Maintains proper spacing and prevents compression during tightening. Central spacer and washers are steel for durability but can be 3D printed for lower-impact applications.
Integrated Pulley Hub
TubeRoller hub integrates a printed pulley for direct power transmission. Recessed nuts allow button-head bolts to lock the polycarbonate tube to the end plugs so tube and roller rotate as one unit.

Key Version History

V1 — ⅜-16 Bolt, Face Contact
Proof of concept. Bearing races in face contact with hub created friction. Functional but not optimized.
V2/V3 — ¼-20 Bolt, TPU Hub
Switched to ¼-20 bolt. V3 introduced TPU material in hub for better impact absorption and misalignment tolerance. Bearing face contact still present — the primary friction source targeted for V4.
V3 — TPU Hub with Face Contact Identified
TPU hub improved compliance. Bearing face contact fully identified as friction source and addressed in V4.
V4 — Final (No Face Contact + Foam Flange)
Carefully placed spacers and washers eliminate all bearing face contact — bearings spin freely. Foam flange add-on prevents lateral foam shift under load. PLA flanges for minimal-impact areas.
