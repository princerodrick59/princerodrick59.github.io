Design Rationale

A fixed-angle hood at 25° was calculated to be optimal for shots from any distance except immediately adjacent to the hub. This eliminates the need for a variable hood mechanism, reducing complexity and failure modes.

Steel flywheel gears maintain a high Moment of Inertia (8.28) to resist speed changes as each Fuel piece is fed — ensuring consistent exit velocity shot to shot.

TPU wedges inside the shooter assist in centering each piece of Fuel before it contacts the flywheel, improving shot consistency and reducing off-axis impacts.

Why Fixed Angle?
1
Simplicity
No variable hood mechanism means fewer moving parts, fewer failure modes, and no adjustment needed between matches.
2
Consistent Velocity
High-MOI steel flywheel gears resist speed drop-off as each ball feeds, ensuring repeatable exit velocity across an entire hopper.
3
Turret Handles Aim
Because the turret provides full 360° pointing, the shooter only needs to cover a range of distances — angle adjustment is not needed.
