1792 TubeRoller System

A custom standardized dead-axle roller system used on every roller of the robot. Goal: a 3D-printable, competition-durable roller compatible with PVC or polycarbonate tubes, fully parametric for any robot.

TubeRoller System — How It Works
Structural Bolt (Not a Shaft)
A ¼-20 bolt acts as the structural member instead of a traditional dead axle shaft — significantly reducing weight and eliminating shaft bending risk under load.
Full-Length Internal Spacer
Maintains proper spacing and prevents compression during tightening. Central spacer and washers are steel for durability but can be 3D printed for lower-impact applications.
Integrated Pulley Hub
TubeRoller hub integrates a printed pulley for direct power transmission. Recessed nuts allow button-head bolts to lock the polycarbonate tube to the end plugs so tube and roller rotate as one unit. Bearings are supported with spacers and washers — no face contact on the races.
V1 — ⅜-16 Bolt, Face Contact
Proof of concept. Bearing races in face contact with hub created friction. Functional but not optimized.
V2/V3 — ¼-20 Bolt, TPU Hub
Switched to ¼-20 bolt. V3 introduced TPU material in hub for better impact absorption. Bearing face contact still present — identified as friction source for V4.
V4 — Final (No Face Contact + Foam Flange)
Carefully placed spacers and washers eliminate all bearing face contact — bearings spin freely. Foam flange add-on prevents lateral foam shift under load. PLA flanges for minimal-impact areas.
