Design Rationale

Foam-wrapped rollers provide compliance for consistent "touch it, own it" acquisition. The bent polycarbonate bull bar stiffens the front and transfers impacts into the structure rather than the roller.

Dead axle pivot removes torsion loading from the pivot shafts. Dead axle rollers eliminate shafts through the roller body, reducing weight and failure modes.

A jackshaft ensures both arms move in perfect sync. SRPP used for all load-bearing components.

1792 TubeRoller System

Every roller on the robot uses our custom standardized dead-axle TubeRoller system — a 3D-printable, competition-durable roller compatible with PVC or polycarbonate tubes, fully parametric for any robot.

A ¼-20 bolt replaces a traditional shaft, eliminating bending risk and reducing weight. Carefully spaced washers eliminate bearing face contact so rollers spin freely. The integrated pulley hub enables direct power transmission without additional components.

Iteration History
V1 — Polybelt PVC Roller
Our initial intake design utilized a PVC roller coated with polybelt material. While the system functioned, it did not meet the performance expectations defined by our "touch it, own it" philosophy, as acquisition was not as consistent or immediate as required. Additionally, this design relied on polybelt — a material we had limited experience with — which introduced uncertainty in tuning, durability, and overall system reliability.
V2 — Foam Rollers
To address durability issues in our initial design, we replaced the PVC roller with a polycarbonate roller, significantly improving impact resistance and eliminating cracking under load. Drawing inspiration from Team 254's 2025 intake, we implemented foam-wrapped rollers using insulation foam commonly found in refrigeration lines. This increased compliance at the intake interface, resulting in more reliable and immediate control of the game piece. Skateboard tape was applied to the intake ramp to increase friction and reduce slip during acquisition.

Structurally, the intake was primarily constructed from SRPP to maintain a lightweight profile, while a bent polycarbonate front "bull bar" was incorporated to increase stiffness and absorb frontal impacts — ensuring impacts were transferred to the structure rather than the roller. The final assembly weighed approximately 8.5 pounds and was designed as a fully passive extension, reducing system complexity while maintaining high performance.
V3 — Final
All primary geometry from V2 was retained; however, we introduced a pivoting element to improve Fuel agitation and ensure more consistent flow out of the hopper. This mechanism helps prevent stagnation and promotes continuous feeding into downstream systems. The pivot is driven through a jackshaft, ensuring synchronized motion between both arms and maintaining even, controlled rotation across the system.
