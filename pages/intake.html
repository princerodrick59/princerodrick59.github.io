Design Rationale

The slap-down intake acquires fuel from the ground and fills the hopper. Foam-wrapped rollers provide compliance for consistent "touch it, own it" acquisition.

The bent polycarbonate bull bar stiffens the front and transfers impacts into the structure rather than the roller. Dead axle pivot removes torsion loading from the pivot shafts. Dead axle rollers eliminate shafts running through the roller body — reducing weight and failure modes.

A jackshaft ensures both intake arms move in perfect sync.

Iteration History
V1 — Polybelt PVC Roller
Initial design used a PVC roller coated with polybelt material. Did not meet "touch it, own it" standards — acquisition was inconsistent. Polybelt was also an unfamiliar material, introducing uncertainty in tuning and durability.
V2 — Foam Rollers (Week 0)
Inspired by Team 254's 2025 intake. Replaced PVC with polycarbonate roller and wrapped in refrigeration-line insulation foam for compliance. Skateboard tape on the intake ramp increased friction and control. Bent polycarbonate bull bar added for front stiffness. ~8.5 lbs, fully passive extension.
V3 — Final (Pivot + Jackshaft)
All V2 geometry retained. Added a pivoting element to agitate Fuel and ensure consistent flow out of the hopper. Jackshaft added to drive both arms in synchronized motion.
