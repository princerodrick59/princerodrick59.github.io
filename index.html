Design Iterations
Krayola CAD — Initial Concept
Established cascading 3-stage with pivoting arm. Used CAD + reef model to determine 93" height. Chose WCP COTS elevator (3-stage capable) over Thrifty Bot.
V1 — Core Structure
Electrical panel added for space utilization + impact protection. Cubic carriage with MAXSpline live axle. Two cameras for AprilTag + coral tracking.
V2 — Weight & Reliability
Square weight reduction failed FEA — rejected. Algae claw removed (strategy change). 3D-printed e-chain replaced with WCP Bi-Directional chain.
Final — FEA-Guided Triangular Cutouts
Triangular cutout pattern reduced elevator by 9 lb. Belly pan → 1/10" SRPP saved 2 lb. Robot reduced from 128 lb to competition weight.

Design Rationale

Carbon fiber chosen because the end effector travels to 93" — every gram saved at height reduces tipping risk and enables lower gear ratios for faster movement.

Gear-driven design eliminates belt tension and chain skip — zero adjustments needed mid-competition.

MAXSpline shaft routes all wiring internally — eliminates wire twist over 720° of rotation.

Iteration History
V1 — "Trident" (Rejected)
Angled front plate for ground pickup. No compliance made ground pickup nearly impossible. Narrow acquisition zone made alignment very difficult.
V2 — 4481-Style Inside Gripper
Inspired by Team 4481 Rembrandts (OpenAlliance). 3D-printed polycarbonate gearset. Spring-loaded algae claw added. MAXComposite SRPP plates on 2.125×1 CF tube.
V3 — Final (Refined 4481 Style)
Algae claw removed (unnecessary weight, strategy revised). Chamfered gear edges reduce edge loading. Final configuration throughout competition season.

Worlds Upgrade — Alignment Funnel

For championships, a funnel was added to reduce cage alignment time. One hard side (aluminum) and one soft side (polycarbonate) pushes the anchor away from the end effector during approach.

Asymmetric compliance guides the cage pipe into the grip teeth even with imprecise approach angles — reducing driver workload in the final 30 seconds.

Iteration History
V1 — Rusthounds RI3D Design (Rejected)
Two MAXSpline pipe design, spring hook, servo release, dyneema winch. Too many moving parts, servo damage, limited clearance with elevator, difficult cage alignment.
V2 — Everybot Door-Hinge Design (Rejected)
Door hinge rotation point, 4-plate polycarbonate one-way gate, ratchet wrench winch. Rejected — packaging conflicts, insufficient frame space.
V3 — Team 2630 Two-Finger Design (Final)
Two fingers engage single cage pipe. ⅛" aluminum superstructure twisted under load — FEA confirmed ¼" needed. Forks iterated to ⅜" steel with geometry matched to cage curvature.

Key Improvements — 2025 vs 2024
CAN Bus Isolation
CANivore separates drivebase CAN from manipulators. Fixed high utilization + reliability issues from 2024.
Motion Magic
CTRE proprietary profiles replace hand-tuned PID. Smoother mechanism control, dramatically faster tuning.
Fused Encoder Class
Fuses absolute encoder + motor position. Mechanisms work even if absolute encoder fails mid-match.
Global Pose Estimation
PhotonVision AprilTags enables pose estimation in teleop (auto-heading) and autonomous (quicker, more consistent).
Autonomous System

PathPlanner generates trajectories. Sensor fusion (beam breaks + ToF) confirms game piece state at each step — robot waits for confirmed intake before proceeding.

Drive-to-pose in development for teleop — driver presses one button to auto-navigate to reef or human player station, reducing cycle time.

Team Structure & Process

Subteam tripled in size in 2025. Co-leaders implemented structured pre-season teaching: motor programming, command-base Java, and "Spinny Carter" ParadeBot as learning platform.

PDSA method: weekly goal checklist → implement → observe → act. Community support via Chief Delphi, Reddit, and FRC Discord.
