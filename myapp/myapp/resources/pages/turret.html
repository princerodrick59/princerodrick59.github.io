Design Rationale

Inspired by Team 987's 2024 turret, the belt-driven design enables shooting from any field position without repositioning the robot. Belt spacing can be adjusted by varying length, allowing flexible packaging.

No dedicated gearbox in the drive path — belts drive directly to the pulley, eliminating backlash and improving positional accuracy for consistent shot angles.

WCP Greyt Turret-inspired stacked bearing arrangement fully constrains and retains the turret across all axes. Wire loom floats from hopper support bars to provide power across all 360° of movement.

Iteration History
V1 — Gear-Driven (Team 1717 Inspired)
Inspired by Team 1717's 2012 robot. Large-diameter gears were more manufacturable than equivalent pulleys given our tooling. Ruled out due to packaging constraints and insufficient gear reduction, impacting integration and controllability.
V2 — Belt-Driven (Team 987 Inspired)
Belt spacing adjustable to fit packaging requirements. WCP Greyt Turret-inspired stacked bearing arrangement provides stability and smooth rotation. Direct belt drive eliminates gearbox backlash for more consistent and responsive turret control. Final configuration throughout the season.
