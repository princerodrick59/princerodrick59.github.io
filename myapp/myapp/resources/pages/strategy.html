Robot Archetypes

Six distinct archetypes were defined across two competition tiers — Einstein and District Event. Each archetype captures the role, capabilities, and priorities a robot needs to fill that alliance slot.

Einstein Tier

E1
Einstein Captain
High-volume scorer maximizing cycle time. Ground intake, shoot-on-the-move, and late-match L1 buzzer climb. Secures the Supercharged RP via 2nd-pick shuttling and manages shuttle duties during inactive periods.
E2
Einstein 1st Pick
Flexible helper with a ground intake that shuttles fuel to the alliance zone all game long. Performs an L1 Climb at the end if the alliance needs the points.
E3
Einstein 2nd Pick
Dedicated shuttler moving fuel from the neutral zone to the alliance zone throughout the match.

District Event Tier

D1
District Captain
L3 Climb, shoot-on-the-move, Energized RP, ground intake. Shuttles during inactive periods.
D2
District 1st Pick
L1 Climb, set-hood shooting, ground intake. Shuttles during inactive, scores during active.
D3
District 2nd Pick
No climb required. Strong defender that can shoot their preload.

MCW Prioritization

After the initial kickoff, we went home to think about strategy, then returned the day after to create our MCW (Must do, Could do, and Won't do) chart. This year, 1792 iterated on our strategy creation process by removing the Should Do portion of the chart that used to exist. This was to create more clarity and simplify the process. We also made a Priority Could Have tier, shown in teal.

What Our Robot Needed to Do
1
Rapid Ground Fuel Acquisition
Quick and reliable ground pickup secures game pieces before opponents can. Fast intake maintains cycle speed and reduces the effectiveness of defensive pressure.
2
High Fuel Storage Capacity
Holding more fuel reduces cycles required and supports completing the Supercharged RP with fewer external dependencies. Maintaining possession keeps game pieces out of opponent control.
3
Strong Autonomous Performance
Scoring as much fuel as possible in auto establishes an early advantage before defensive play begins and increases overall match consistency.
4
High-Speed Fuel Shooting
A fast shooter converts stored game pieces into points quickly, reducing stationary scoring time and maintaining a high scoring rate throughout the match.
