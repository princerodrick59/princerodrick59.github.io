Safety in Our Team Space

Our shop environment is governed by consistent safety procedures to protect every student and mentor during build season and year-round operations.

Safety Procedures

Evacuation Drills
Regular evacuation drills are conducted to ensure all team members know exit routes and assembly points in the event of an emergency.
Safety Talks
A safety talk is held at the beginning of every meeting. Topics rotate through what has been observed in the shop and content from the FIRST Safety Manual, keeping awareness fresh and relevant.

Supervision & PPE

Mentored Work Areas
Mentors supervise the machine shop and all handtool work areas to ensure safe technique and proper equipment use at all times.
Youth Protection Plan
All one-on-one conversations between adults and students require a minimum of two adults present, in compliance with FIRST's Youth Protection Plan.
PPE & Field Behavior
Proper PPE is enforced for all applicable work. Safe field behavior standards are communicated and enforced during robot testing and operation.
Safety Supply Inventory
All safety supplies are tracked in our inventory system to ensure nothing is missing before a meeting or event and replacements are ordered proactively.
Safety in the Pit

Our pit is set up and operated with a defined safety structure — a dedicated Pit Boss keeps the space clean and organized, and a student wearing a Safety Satchel is responsible for safety awareness throughout the event.

Equipment On Hand

Eyewash StationOn-site for immediate eye irrigation
Fire ExtinguisherAccessible within the pit footprint
Battery Spill KitFor safe containment of electrolyte spills
First Aid KitStocked and checked before each event
Material Safety SheetsKept in a dedicated binder in the pit

Procedures & Personnel

Evacuation Plans
Venue and hotel evacuation plans are prepared and reviewed before each event so the whole team knows what to do regardless of location.
Pit Shutdown Checklist
At the end of each day the pit follows a defined shutdown procedure — no batteries in the robot, no lights on, and all equipment secured.
Safety Student
A designated student is responsible for pit safety throughout the event. They can be identified by their Safety Satchel and carry Safety Business Cards linked to our website — including evacuation plans and emergency phone numbers.
Pit Boss
A dedicated Pit Boss maintains cleanliness and organization in the pit throughout the event, reducing trip hazards and keeping the workspace functional under pressure.
Safety in Our Community

Team 1792 extends its safety culture beyond the shop and competition floor into the broader community through active outreach.

National Night Out Presentation
The team participates in National Night Out with a safety-focused presentation, engaging the local community on topics relevant to public safety and emergency preparedness.
Trauma Kits
The team maintains trauma kits available for community use, reinforcing our commitment to safety extending beyond our own events and team activities.
